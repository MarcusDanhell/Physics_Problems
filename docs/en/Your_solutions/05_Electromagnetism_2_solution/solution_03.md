## 3. Biot - Savart Law

To calculate the magnetic field produced by a small current-carrying segment, we use the **Biot-Savart Law**:

$$dB = \frac{\mu_0}{4\pi} \frac{I \, dL \sin(\theta)}{r^2}$$

### 1. Identify the given values:
*   **Current ($I$):** $3 \text{ A}$
*   **Length of segment ($dL$):** $0.1 \text{ m}$
*   **Distance ($r$):** $0.2 \text{ m}$
*   **Angle ($\theta$):** $90^\circ$ (since the segment is perpendicular to the line to point $P$)
*   **Permeability of free space ($\mu_0$):** $4\pi \times 10^{-7} \text{ T}\cdot\text{m/A}$

### 2. Set up the constants:
The constant $\frac{\mu_0}{4\pi}$ simplifies to $10^{-7} \text{ T}\cdot\text{m/A}$.

### 3. Substitution and Calculation:
Since the segment is perpendicular, $\sin(90^\circ) = 1$.

$$B = 10^{-7} \times \frac{3 \times 0.1 \times 1}{(0.2)^2}$$

$$B = 10^{-7} \times \frac{0.3}{0.04}$$

$$B = 10^{-7} \times 7.5$$

### Final Result:
The magnetic field at point $P$ is:
**$$B = 7.5 \times 10^{-7} \text{ T}$$**
(or $0.75 \text{ \muT }$)
