## Force Comparison

### 1. Constants and Given Values
* **Distance ($r$):** $5.3 \times 10^{-11} \text{ m}$
* **Charge of electron/proton ($e$):** $1.60 \times 10^{-19} \text{ C}$
* **Mass of electron ($m_e$):** $9.11 \times 10^{-31} \text{ kg}$
* **Mass of proton ($m_p$):** $1.67 \times 10^{-27} \text{ kg}$
* **Coulomb constant ($k$):** $8.99 \times 10^9 \text{ N}\cdot\text{m}^2/\text{C}^2$
* **Gravitational constant ($G$):** $6.67 \times 10^{-11} \text{ N}\cdot\text{m}^2/\text{kg}^2$

---

### 2. Calculate Electric Force ($F_e$)
Using Coulomb’s Law:
$$F_e = \frac{k |q_1 q_2|}{r^2}$$
$$F_e = \frac{(8.99 \times 10^9)(1.60 \times 10^{-19})^2}{(5.3 \times 10^{-11})^2}$$
$$F_e \approx 8.2 \times 10^{-8} \text{ N}$$

---

### 3. Calculate Gravitational Force ($F_g$)
Using Newton’s Law of Gravitation:
$$F_g = \frac{G m_e m_p}{r^2}$$
$$F_g = \frac{(6.67 \times 10^{-11})(9.11 \times 10^{-31})(1.67 \times 10^{-27})}{(5.3 \times 10^{-11})^2}$$
$$F_g \approx 3.6 \times 10^{-47} \text{ N}$$

---

### 4. Calculate the Ratio ($F_e / F_g$)
To find the relative strength, we divide the two magnitudes:
$$\frac{F_e}{F_g} = \frac{8.2 \times 10^{-8}}{3.6 \times 10^{-47}}$$
$$\frac{F_e}{F_g} \approx 2.27 \times 10^{39}$$

**Conclusion:** The electric force is approximately **$2.27 \times 10^{39}$ times stronger** than the gravitational force. This demonstrates why gravity is considered negligible when dealing with subatomic particles.
