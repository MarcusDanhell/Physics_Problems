## Electric Potential

### 1. Identify the Variables
* **Charges:** $q_1 = +1\text{ C}$, $q_2 = -2\text{ C}$, $q_3 = +3\text{ C}$, $q_4 = -4\text{ C}$
* **Side length ($a$):** $1.0\text{ m}$
* **Coulomb's Constant ($k$):** $\approx 8.99 \times 10^9\text{ N}\cdot\text{m}^2/\text{C}^2$

### 2. Calculate the Distance to the Center
The distance ($r$) from any corner of a square to its center is half the length of the diagonal. Using the Pythagorean theorem for a square with side $a$:
$$\text{Diagonal} = a\sqrt{2} = 1.0\sqrt{2}\text{ m}$$
$$r = \frac{a\sqrt{2}}{2} = \frac{1.0\sqrt{2}}{2} \approx 0.707\text{ m}$$

### 3. Apply the Electric Potential Formula
The electric potential $V$ at a distance $r$ from a point charge $q$ is given by:
$$V = \frac{kq}{r}$$

Since all charges are at the same distance $r$ from the center, the total potential $V_{total}$ is:
$$V_{total} = \frac{k}{r} (q_1 + q_2 + q_3 + q_4)$$

### 4. Final Calculation
Substitute the values into the equation:
$$V_{total} = \frac{8.99 \times 10^9}{0.707} (1 - 2 + 3 - 4)$$
$$V_{total} = \frac{8.99 \times 10^9}{0.707} (-2)$$
$$V_{total} \approx -2.54 \times 10^{10}\text{ V}$$

**The electric potential at the center of the square is approximately $-2.54 \times 10^{10}\text{ Volts}$.**
