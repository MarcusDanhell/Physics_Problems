## Field Levitation

### 1. Identify the Forces
* **Gravitational Force ($F_g$):** $F_g = m_p g$
* **Electric Force ($F_e$):** $F_e = qE$ (where $q = e$)

### 2. Set Up the Equilibrium Equation
For levitation (net force = 0):
$$F_e = F_g$$
$$eE = m_p g$$

### 3. Solve for Electric Field Strength ($E$)
$$E = \frac{m_p g}{e}$$

### 4. Substitute the Values
* **Mass of proton ($m_p$):** $1.67 \times 10^{-27} \text{ kg}$
* **Gravitational acceleration ($g$):** $9.8 \text{ m/s}^2$
* **Charge of proton ($e$):** $1.6 \times 10^{-19} \text{ C}$

$$E = \frac{(1.67 \times 10^{-27})(9.8)}{1.6 \times 10^{-19}}$$
$$E \approx \frac{1.6366 \times 10^{-26}}{1.6 \times 10^{-19}}$$
$$E \approx 1.02 \times 10^{-7} \text{ N/C}$$

**The required electric field strength is approximately $1.02 \times 10^{-7} \text{ N/C}$ (directed upwards).**
