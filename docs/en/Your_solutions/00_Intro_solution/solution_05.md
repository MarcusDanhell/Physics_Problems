
### 1. The Geometry of the Vector
Imagine the vector as the hypotenuse of a right-angled triangle.
* The **magnitude (15)** is the length of the hypotenuse.
* The **angle ($60^\circ$)** is between the hypotenuse and the horizontal base.
* The **horizontal component ($A_x$)** is the adjacent side.
* The **vertical component ($A_y$)** is the opposite side.



### 2. Using Trigonometry
We use the Sine and Cosine functions because they relate the angles of a triangle to the lengths of its sides:

* **Horizontal ($A_x$):** Uses Cosine (Adjacent/Hypotenuse)
    $$A_x = A \cos(\theta) = 15 \cdot \cos(60^\circ)$$
    Since $\cos(60^\circ) = 0.5$, then $A_x = 15 \cdot 0.5 = \mathbf{7.5}$

* **Vertical ($A_y$):** Uses Sine (Opposite/Hypotenuse)
    $$A_y = A \sin(\theta) = 15 \cdot \sin(60^\circ)$$
    Since $\sin(60^\circ) = \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2} \approx 0.866$, then $A_y = 15 \cdot 0.866 \approx \mathbf{12.99}$
