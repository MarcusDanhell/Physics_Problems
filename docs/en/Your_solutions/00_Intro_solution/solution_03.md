
### 1. The Proportionality Rules
* **Direct Proportionality ($m_1, m_2$):** Force is directly proportional to the product of the masses. if you cut a mass in half, the force cuts in half.
* **Inverse-Square Law ($r^2$):** Force is inversely proportional to the *square* of the distance. This means if you double the distance, the force doesn't just halve; it drops by $2^2$ (which is 4).

### 2. The Step-by-Step Calculation
Let’s apply the changes mentioned in your prompt:

1.  **Mass 1 is halved:** Multiply the force by $1/2$.
2.  **Mass 2 is halved:** Multiply the force again by $1/2$.
3.  **Distance is doubled:** Since $r$ is squared in the denominator ($1/r^2$), doubling $r$ means we multiply the force by $1/2^2$, which is $1/4$.



**The Final Factor:**
$$\text{New Force} = \left( \frac{1}{2} \right) \times \left( \frac{1}{2} \right) \times \left( \frac{1}{4} \right) = \mathbf{\frac{1}{16}}$$
The gravitational pull becomes **16 times weaker** than it was originally.
