
### 1. The Relationship
Think of these as a chain of change:
* **Position ($\vec{r}$):** Where the object is.
* **Velocity ($\vec{v}$):** How fast the position is changing ($\frac{d\vec{r}}{dt}$).
* **Acceleration ($\vec{a}$):** How fast the velocity is changing ($\frac{d\vec{v}}{dt}$).

### 2. The Step-by-Step Derivation
Given the position vector:
$$\vec{r}(t) = (3t^2)\hat{i} + (5t - 8t^2)\hat{j}$$

**Finding Velocity ($\vec{v}$):**
Take the derivative of each component with respect to time ($t$).
* Derivative of $3t^2$ is $6t$.
* Derivative of $5t - 8t^2$ is $5 - 16t$.
$$\vec{v}(t) = (6t)\hat{i} + (5 - 16t)\hat{j}$$

**Finding Acceleration ($\vec{a}$):**
Take the derivative of the velocity components.
* Derivative of $6t$ is $6$.
* Derivative of $5 - 16t$ is $-16$.
$$\vec{a}(t) = 6\hat{i} - 16\hat{j}$$
