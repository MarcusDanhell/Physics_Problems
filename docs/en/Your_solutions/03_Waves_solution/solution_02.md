2. Electric Potential
Electric potential $V$ is a scalar quantity, calculated as $V = \sum \frac{kq_i}{r}$.
Distance ($r$): As calculated above, $r = \frac{\sqrt{2}}{2} \approx 0.707\text{ m}$.
Constant ($k$): $8.99 \times 10^9\text{ N}\cdot\text{m}^2/\text{C}^2$.
Sum of charges: $Q_{total} = (+1) + (-2) + (+3) + (-4) = -2\text{ C}$.$$V = \frac{k}{r} \sum q_i = \frac{8.99 \times 10^9}{0.707} \times (-2) \approx -2.54 \times 10^{10}\text{ V}$$
